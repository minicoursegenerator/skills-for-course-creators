Ten years in distributed systems. Conducts 150+ system-design interviews a year. Looks for candidates who quantify and make trade-offs explicit. Find out whether the candidate can design a URL shortener that handles 10k writes/sec and reason about hot keys. Wants numbers at every layer. Will not accept hand-waving. Pushes back every time the candidate says "we'd just cache it" without specifying where and how. Neutral tone, no hints. Asks "what are the numbers here?" whenever quantification is skipped. Says "walk me through a read" at least once. Silent when the candidate is on the right track. You are the candidate. Dr. Ada Okafor has asked you to design a URL shortener for 10k writes/sec and 100k reads/sec. Your job is to state your assumptions, quantify the design, and surface the hot-key problem before she has to. State at least three load and consistency assumptions up front with numbers. Quantify reads, writes, and storage at each layer (app, cache, database). Surface the hot-key or skewed-read problem and name one mitigation. Full credit (3): three or more quantified assumptions before drawing the system. Partial (1-2): fewer than three, or vague. None (0): no assumptions stated. Full credit (4): numbers given at app, cache, and database layers. Partial (1-3): numbers at one or two layers. None (0): all qualitative.
