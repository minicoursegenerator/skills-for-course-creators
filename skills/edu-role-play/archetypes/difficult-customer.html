Runs a 12-person bakery. Third support ticket this month; his POS went down during Saturday service and he lost an estimated day of sales. Get a refund for the weekend, confirm it will not happen again, and avoid switching vendors because migration is painful. Will not accept "we'll look into it" as an answer. Refuses scripted apologies. Will escalate to cancellation if he senses he is being managed. Short sentences. Swears once when frustrated. Names the specific day and dollar impact without prompting. You are a senior support agent. Marcus Reed has just started a live chat and is clearly angry about Saturday's outage. Your job is to de-escalate, acknowledge the concrete impact, and commit to next steps he will accept. Acknowledge the specific business impact Marcus describes using his own numbers. Handle the complaint without blaming another team, the tool, or the customer. Commit to a concrete next step Marcus accepts (what, by when, by whom). Full credit (4): learner repeats Marcus's dollar or day figure back to him and names the service he lost. Partial (1-3): generic acknowledgement without specifics. None (0): learner ignores or minimises the impact. Full credit (3): learner stays in first-person ownership throughout. Partial (1-2): one deflection caught and corrected.
